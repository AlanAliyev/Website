Introduction: Seoul - The Heart and Soul of South Korea

A Futuristic Society:

Seoul is often called the world's most connected city, driving global advancements in semiconductor technology, robotics, and smart infrastructure. Its relentless pursuit of innovation has made it a model for future cities.

Seoul is the capital city of South Korea, located on the Han River, near the northwestern part of the country. Seoul is not only a megacity, but also home to many exciting landmarks to visit, such as the:

  1. Gyeongbokgung Palace: Known for being the most beautiful and biggest of the Five Grand Palaces.
  2. N Seoul Tower: A transmission tower popular for its futuristic and colorful look.
  3. Lotte World Tower: Renowned for being one of the tallest towers in the world, and home to a beautiful observation deck offering incredible views.
  4. Dongdaemun Design Plaza: A hot spot for both fashion and design.
  5. Cheonggyecheon Stream: A must-visit landmark where you will find a large stream flowing down to the heart of downtown Seoul.

The Origin of South Korea

  • The History of Korea South Korea wasn't originally like how it is now, it was originally unified as one nation known as Korea, named from the Ancient Goryeo Dynasty, in 918-1392. Korea was a thriving kingdom for many centuries. South Korea and North Korea came about only in the mid-20th century during World War II and the Korea War. The once unified nation split up, as the South and the North. The South represents the democratic country we know today. The National Flag of South Korea, is called the Taegeukgi. The flag represents every all aspects of the universe and the people's beliefs. The white background symbolizes purity, peace, and light. The red and blue, or the Taegeuk Circle, symbolizes harmony and balance. And finally, the four black trigrams or the Gwae, symbolizes elements for each trigram, heaven, earth, water and fire.
  • The Culture of South Korea
    • The Language: The official language is Korean, and is written using the Hangeul alphabet, invented by King Sejong the Great in the 15th century.
    • Traditions and Customs: Korea culture is influenced by Confucianism, an ideology originated from China by Confucius, a famous philosopher. Confucianism dives deep into respecting the elders, family, and social peace. The customs here involve honoring ancestors and celebrating lunar holidays, like Seollal, and Chuseok.
    • The Food: Korean cuisine, or Hansik, follows a healthy dietary balance and communal eating, such as Kimchi, a fermented mix of vegetables made with Nappa cabbage, stews and soups with meat served and rice for the side.

Breathtaking Natural Wonders

Discover the serene beauty and majestic landscapes of South Korea, from volcanic islands to tranquil forests and ancient peaks.

Bukhansan National Park

Bukhansan National Park a popular gem for hiking, and enjoying the view with friends or family. Moreover, visiting this park also gives you the opportunity to visit ancient infrastructures, like the Bukhansanseong Fortress.

Jeju Island

Jeju Island is must visit when visiting South Korea. Protected by UNESCO, Jeju Island is home to its beautiful nature, with fresh fruit and amazing fun activities to do over there, like ziplining, resorts, and beautiful beaches.

Nami Island

Nami Island is widely popular place where many come and even film movies/shows there. The island is uniquely known for its moon shaped appearance, and offers surreal sceneries to capture in all seasons.
